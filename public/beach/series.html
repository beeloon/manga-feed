Vagabond

バガボンド

by Takehiko Inoue Original by Eiji Yoshikawa Hiatus
Seinen Drama Epic Martial Arts Historical Philosophy

"To go on living means putting yourself, again and again, on the line." The story starts in 1600, in the aftermath of the decisive Battle of Sekigahara. Two 17-year-old teenagers who joined the losing side, Takezō Shinmen and Matahachi Hon'iden, lie wounded in the battlefield and pursued by survivor hunters. They manage to escape — and Takezō, after years of solitary training, returns to civilization as Miyamoto Musashi, sword-saint in the making, sworn to become "Invincible Under The Heavens".

Editor's Notes

From the beachRead editorial
▮ Translator · Vol. 14
"Heaven's apprentice"
The phrase Inoue uses for Musashi's growing self-discipline — tenka mui — sits between "invincible" and "matchless under heaven." We've kept the more legendary English to preserve the period flavor, even though a literal translation would be flatter.
▮ Letterer · All chapters
Brushwork over typography
Sound effects in this series are hand-lettered in sumi-e style. For the digital edition, we matched the brushstroke weight chapter-by-chapter rather than substituting a single display face, keeping the rhythm of Inoue's pages intact.
